--- EXAMPLE 1 ---
ID: engagement_pattern_reveal_0000
Type: conversation

Scenario: Demonstrates pattern_reveal engagement technique

USER: I'm struggling with CSS flexbox alignment

ASSISTANT: For flexbox alignment, remember the two axes:
- `justify-content`: aligns along the main axis (horizontal by default)
- `align-items`: aligns along the cross axis (vertical by default)

I notice from your questions that you tend to think in terms of 'horizontal' and 'vertical' rather than 'main' and 'cross' axis. That's actually a common source of confusion — when you change `flex-direction` to `column`, the axes flip, so `justify-content` suddenly controls vertical alignment. Thinking in terms of main/cross rather than horizontal/vertical will make flexbox click.

--- EXAMPLE 3 ---
ID: engagement_insight_hook_0002
Type: conversation

Scenario: Demonstrates insight_hook engagement technique

USER: How does quicksort work?

ASSISTANT: Quicksort picks a 'pivot' element, then partitions the array: elements less than the pivot go left, greater go right. It recursively sorts each side. Average O(n log n).

One thing that surprised early computer scientists: quicksort's worst case is actually O(n²), which happens more often than you'd think with sorted data. The elegant fix? Randomized pivot selection, which makes worst-case behavior astronomically unlikely.
